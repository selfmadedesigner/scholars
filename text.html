Luz Mely Reyes is an award-winning investigative journalist and advocate for press freedom in Latin America. With over 25 years of experience, she has become one of Venezuela’s most respected voices in political journalism. Her career began dramatically in 1992, when she covered a coup attempt on her very first day as a reporter. Since then, she has held key editorial roles at major Venezuelan newspapers, including Últimas Noticias and Diario 2001, where she became the first woman to serve as editor-in-chief of a national paper.


In 2015, Reyes co-founded Efecto Cocuyo, a pioneering independent news platform created in response to growing censorship and repression in Venezuela. Under her leadership, the outlet has become a beacon for transparent, fact-based journalism, known for its investigative reporting and commitment to human rights. Despite facing government harassment and threats, Reyes continues to champion journalistic integrity and freedom of expression.


Beyond her newsroom work, Reyes is also a published author, educator, and international speaker. She has received numerous accolades, including the 2018 International Press Freedom Award from the Committee to Protect Journalists. Her story is one of resilience, courage, and an unwavering belief in the power of journalism to illuminate truth and empower society
